Baking Recorded

All things great and chocolate! - Read Time: 8 minuets

"Cooking is both physical and mental therapy" - Mary Berry

1.Brownies Galore - the best chocolate dessert on earth. There is nothing better on this planet in my opinion than a good chocolate brownie. Nothing. Those who say otherwise (unless alergic to chocolate - yes, I knew someone with this allergen!) is a LIAR! What makes the best chocolate brownie? Is it the soft crunch on the top? The lovely and soft chocolate rich middle? Or is it the comaprison between the hotness of a brownie and the coolness of ice-cream when the two are paired together? In my opinon it is all! If it is the soft crunch thats best for you, I point you towards a cookie. If it is just the gooey middle, chocolate fudge cake. Most desserts go well with icecream. So, case and point really!

2. Chocolate Mousse- a light, fluffy and smooth rich delicacy. A delightful dish, perfect for the summer. Its chocolate and its cold. Its light so its the perfect ending for a dinner that has *almost* filled you up, but not quiet! There are many ways to enjoy this dessert. Wether its a chocolate and raspberry mousse, chocolate orange or perhaps it is inside a profiterole? Whatever takes your fancy, you best believe that this dessert is just as tasty as a brownie. Personally however, brownies is better hence the second place.

3. Chocolate Cake - a gateau or a red velvet? Another chocolate dessert I know! Similar to brownie, I am aware! But, chocolate cake for me is nostalgic. It was made and prepared for me by someone who was very close to me. I enjoyed looking forward to chocolate cake because I knew I would be seeing the person who made the chocolate cake. Sometimes, there is nothing better than comfort food. It is good for the heart.

Cooking crimes of tastiness

The tantilisng journey of putting food on the table! - Read Time: 6 minuets

What are some of the best foods that you always want to eat? For me alot of the dinners I look forwards to always feature pasta or rice. The Italians know how to cook food that is for sure. I am nnot a huge fan of seafoods, so however much I may crave a seafood linguini I am sad to say I wouldn't enjoy it. But, any pasta that has a meat base like a good old' ragu spaghetti bolognase I love. Lasagne, is one of my favourites, I love the layers that go into the dish giving it a nice structure. Raviolli is also delish, one of my favourite combiaitions is a spinach and ricotta ravolli. Which is good because who doesn't love veggies and cheese.

Asian foods have spectacular flavour ranges, and one genre of dish that I love is curry. There are so many different curries and when I go out to eat at an Indian restaurant I try to make sure I taste a new curry. Some of the curry dishes I love are: Bhunas, Baltis and Masala. Butter chicken I discovered through ticktok, the colour of this dish instantly compelled me to try it. However, I didn't enjoy this dish as much as other ones as for me I found that it was too sweet and too buttery. This may just be the restaurant I had it at though, so I will definately be trying it again!

Other rice dishes I enjoy include paella or just simply steamed rice and fish. For me a seafood paella is not my favourite and I will avoid it because of the seafood that typically goes into it. But a vegetable paella is fantastic. The blend of the rice and the vegetables with a side of calamaris make it one of the best holdiay meals to have when one the beachfront.

Comfort food for me takes form as a stew, soup, shepards pie or a roast dinner. In the cold and miserable english winters hot warming food, with potatoes on the side (my fave), cannot be beaten! Stew is what i look forward to about winter because its too warming to eat at other times!
